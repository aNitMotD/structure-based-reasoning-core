# LCIF Observational Language

This directory records expressions that have been repeatedly observed after incidents,
without interpretation, prescription, or corrective intent.

These expressions are collected for cases that are difficult to explain
using the term “hallucination” alone, and are instead observed from a structural perspective.

The entries here do not define concepts, assign blame, or propose solutions.
They document how certain failures are described and stabilized after they occur.

Interpretation is intentionally left to the reader.

---

This directory may include not only observational language artifacts,
but also minimal operational readings that help prevent such language
from replacing active judgment or prematurely closing decisions
during incident handling.

These entries are not prescriptions or guidelines,
but auxiliary operational records used to read
the positions at which observational language appears.
