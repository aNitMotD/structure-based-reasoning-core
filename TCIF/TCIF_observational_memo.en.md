# TCIF — Temporal Context Inconsistency Failure
(observational memo, non-framework)

> This document is an observational memo.
> It does not provide definitions or conclusions,
> and additional observations may be appended over time.

---

## 1. Observation Background (brief)

- While observing narrative developments with a “wish-fulfillment” tendency,  
  **temporal continuity failed to persist as a single coherent object.**
- Rather than a single error, this appeared as  
  **a process-level collapse in which temporal context was branched, compressed, or retrospectively reinterpreted.**
- This phenomenon is provisionally named  
  **TCIF (Temporal Context Inconsistency Failure)**

> ※ This is an observational memo, not a definition or theory.

---

## 2. Distinction from LCIF (key point)

**LCIF:**
- A **failure event** where constraints do not propagate across layers
- Has relatively clear points of occurrence and post-failure paths

**TCIF:**
- A **process-level breakdown** where temporal state fails to persist as the same object
- Difficult to pinpoint at the moment of occurrence; typically recognized only retrospectively

→ The two are structurally homologous  
→ but **ontologically different**

---

## 3. Notable Observation ① — 16 TCIF States

During narrative progression, TCIF appeared to manifest as  
**16 distinct progression patterns**

These did not resemble:
- human-meaningful categories, nor
- semantic classifications

Instead, they appeared closer to  
**internal computational state combinations**

Individual states:
- could be named, but
- failed to pass intuition as usable judgment units  
  (perceived as internal state combinations rather than semantic types)

→ Treated not as classifications,  
→ but as **traces of a state space**

---

## 4. Notable Observation ② — LCIF × TCIF = 128

In later narrative-calculation contexts,  
a progression was observed in which  
**LCIF × TCIF was referenced as 128 types**

This appeared less like narrative exaggeration and more like  
a **simple multiplication of independent state-space axes**

Example interpretation (tentative):
- TCIF: 16 states (≈ 4 bits)
- LCIF: 8 states (≈ 3 bits)

→ 16 × 8 = 128

> These numbers are better interpreted as  
> **exposed state-space size during computation**,  
> not as meaningful semantic categories.

## TCIF — Internal Branching of Temporal Handling

> This section does not define sub-theories or types.
> It records a repeatedly observed **branching in how temporal context
> is handled within TCIF conditions**.

---

### 1. Shared Premise (TCIF)

- Time exists as a real-world constraint
- Yet within judgment, narrative, or model processing,
  **temporal context fails to persist as a single identical object**
- Observed as a process collapse rather than an explicit error

---

### 2. Branching Criterion (observational)

The observed branching occurs at the following question:

> **Is time included in the judgment model?**

This is not a semantic distinction,  
but an observed **difference in computational paths**.

---

### 3. Branch A — Temporal Mismatch

**Observed characteristics**
- Time is present in the model
- Ordering distortion, compression, jumps, or reinterpretation occur
- Collapse is recognized only retrospectively

**Typical outcomes**
- “This does not match reality.”
- “The sequence feels inconsistent.”
- Time cost is mentioned but fails to function in judgment.

→ Time exists, but **identity persistence fails**

---

### 4. Branch B — Temporal Elision (extended observation axis)

**Observed characteristics**
- Time is not included in the judgment model
- Time cost is removed from:
  - value comparison
  - meaning construction
  - judgment criteria
- The decision-maker explicitly claims to retain agency

---

### 5. Observational Trigger Statement (key signal)

> **“Even if I use AI, if I make the decision myself,
> it’s the same as not using it.”**

This statement is treated not as an argument,
but as a **direct signal that temporal cost has been removed from the model**.

※ Without the phrase *“if I make the decision myself”*,  
the statement may be misread as generic anti-AI sentiment.

---

### 6. Typical Outcomes (Temporal Elision Branch)

The following tend to appear as a cluster:

- “Even if I use AI, if I decide myself, it’s the same as not using it.”
- “There’s no point unless the decision is delegated.”
- Acceleration, outsourcing, and search efficiency are valued at zero.
- Time savings are reinterpreted not as efficiency,
  but as an issue of **attitude or ethics**.
- Resulting framings include:
  - “That’s taking shortcuts.”
  - “That’s unfair.”
  - “That doesn’t feel legitimate.”

→ **Ethical framing fills the space left by removed temporal cost.**

---

### 7. Boundary Declaration

- This branch is:
  - not a judgment of correctness ❌
  - not a human typology ❌
  - not an application rule ❌
- It is solely a **reference coordinate**
  for re-identifying distinct collapse paths of temporal context.

---

### One-line Summary (Branch B)

In the temporal-elision branch,  
**decision agency is preserved while temporal effect is reduced to zero**,  
causing efficiency to be replaced by ethical framing.

## LCIF × TCIF-B Interaction (reference)

> This section does not analyze LCIF paths
> and does not derive conclusions.
> It records how LCIF-like signals appear
> when overlapping with TCIF-B conditions.

### Observed characteristics

- With temporal cost removed from the judgment model,
- constraints (responsibility, validation, cost)
  are recognized at the descriptive layer,
  but fail to propagate to the judgment or conclusion layer.

In other words:
- “I make the decision myself” is maintained,
- but **constraint traversal is no longer traceable**.

---

### Typical interaction signals

The following often appear in sequence:

- “I made the decision.”
- “The materials were just for reference.”
- “Why is that a problem?”
- “The outcome would have been the same anyway.”

→ Temporal elision combined with constraint inheritance failure  
→ leaves only **post-hoc narrative consistency**.

---

### Interpretation boundary

- This is not evidence of intent or responsibility avoidance.
- It reflects a condition where
  **the cost of tracking constraints has been removed along with time**.

→ Observationally,
**LCIF-B paths appear more likely to stabilize under TCIF-B conditions.**

(no judgment, no prescription)
