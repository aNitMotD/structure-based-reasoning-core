## Meta: Role–Cognition Separation

- Principle:
  Role and Cognition belong to different layers.

- Structure:
  Cognitive Engine (invariant)
        ↓
  Role / Context (variable)
        ↓
  Output (observable)

- Constraint:
  Cognition cannot be directly inferred from Role.

- Common Failure:
  Role–Cognition Collapse  
  → interpreting role as cognition

- Cause:
  - Observability limit (internal cognition is not visible)
  - Minimal inference model (simplified mapping)
  - Temporal alignment assumption (synchronizing role and cognition in time)

- Example (QA Case):
  - Observation:
    “The user has been working as QA since 2020.”

  - Common Interpretation:
    “The user’s thinking has been shaped or reorganized into a QA-oriented mindset since 2020.”

  - Structural Correction:
    The cognitive engine exists prior to the role.  
    The QA role only constrains how the existing cognitive engine is expressed.

- Implication:
  Role does not define cognition.  
  It only constrains the form of output.

- Boundary:
  Cognition is not directly observable.  
  Role is only an observable constraint on output.
  
- Correlation Clause:
  Observable role history may correlate with output patterns.
  However, that correlation does not justify direct inference or determination of internal cognitive structure.

- Non-Inference Clause:
  Internal cognitive structure cannot be determined or asserted  
  from observable output alone.

- Summary:
  Role does not create cognition.  
  It only constrains how cognition is expressed.

- Final Note:
  Method-Trait is also structurally isomorphic.
