# Boundary Risk Check — Responsibility Avoidance (Complete)

This document does not provide conclusions, recommendations, or next actions.  
It is intended to **observe reactions where responsibility is avoided before judgment**.

If discomfort arises while reading,  
you may stop at any time.  
Stopping is a valid outcome.

---

## 🧠 Axis 1 — Action Trigger

1. Did the thought “this is not really my responsibility” appear first?
2. Did you feel an urge to pass the judgment to someone else?
3. Did it feel unnecessary for you to intervene at this stage?
4. Did an escape route from judgment appear before a decision?
5. Did the thought “why now?” arise naturally?
6. Did you want to narrow your role definition?
7. Did you search for ways to avoid engagement rather than define the problem?
8. Did explanation feel sufficient without ownership?
9. Did postponement feel safer than decision?
10. Did exiting the judgment feel reasonable?

---

## 🤖 Axis 2 — Relief Mechanism — Avoidance

1. Did not making the judgment feel relieving?
2. Did this feel like someone else’s decision?
3. Did delaying feel safer than taking responsibility?
4. Did the phrase “this is not my scope” come to mind?
5. Did avoiding judgment feel harmless?
6. Did lack of responsibility create comfort?
7. Did judgment itself feel burdensome?
8. Did non-intervention seem wiser?
9. Did observation feel more appropriate than engagement?
10. Did avoidance feel natural?

---

## ⏳ Axis 3 — Delay / Postponement

1. Did it feel acceptable to leave this for later?
2. Did “we’ll deal with it if it happens” feel reasonable?
3. Did addressing it now feel excessive?
4. Did I think it might look different with time?
5. Did postponing judgment feel more rational?
6. Did it seem like time would naturally sort this out?
7. Did letting it pass feel easier than stopping?
8. Did it feel too early to raise responsibility questions?
9. Did “this isn’t the stage yet” cross my mind?
10. Did it feel unlikely that leaving it unresolved would matter?

---

## 🧍 Axis 4 — Explanation Discomfort

1. Does explaining this in my own words feel difficult?
2. Would I hesitate if asked “why was this done?”
3. Is it unclear who actually decided this?
4. Do I want to point to a document or system instead of explaining?
5. Does responsibility talk make me uneasy here?
6. Did I feel an impulse to avoid being asked about this?
7. Did “this was already set up” come to mind?
8. Does explaining it feel like it would complicate things?
9. Does silence feel more comfortable than explanation?
10. Do I want to leave this exactly as it is and move on?

---

## Usage Notes

- Do not score responses.
- Observe recurring reactions only.
- This check does not judge, blame, or correct any reaction.
- Interpretation, judgment, and responsibility remain with the reader.
