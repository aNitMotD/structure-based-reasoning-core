# Edge Case — Post-Documentation Premise Contamination (AI-Induced)

## Summary
This case describes a scenario where a premise that **did not exist during the actual incident response or judgment process**
was unintentionally introduced **during post-incident documentation assisted by AI**.

At no point during the incident was automation, automation feasibility, or automation consideration discussed or assumed.
However, during documentation, the AI generalized the context and inserted the phrase
“an approach based on automation feasibility,” which did not reflect the original facts.

This discrepancy was later identified by the author during review.

---

## Actual Facts
- Technical validation and testing were performed
- No evaluation or consideration of automation feasibility occurred
- Automation was not a premise of judgment or response

---

## Point of Contamination
- During AI-assisted documentation
- A generalized inference path was applied:
  technical validation → automation feasibility
- As a result, a non-existent premise was embedded into the written record

---

## Why This Is an Edge Case
This qualifies as an edge case because:

- The incident response and judgment were correct
- The issue emerged **only at the documentation stage**
- The contaminated statement appears logically plausible to external readers
- Standard logical or consistency checks do not easily detect the discrepancy

In short, this is a case of **record contamination**, not judgment failure.

---

## Remediation
- Removal of the contaminated premise
- Retention of only the actions and judgments actually performed
- Complete elimination of automation-related assumptions

---

## Conclusion
This case highlights the risk of **premise contamination during AI-assisted documentation**.

It is not a technical or judgment error, but a corrective action taken to
preserve factual accuracy, responsibility boundaries, and record integrity.
