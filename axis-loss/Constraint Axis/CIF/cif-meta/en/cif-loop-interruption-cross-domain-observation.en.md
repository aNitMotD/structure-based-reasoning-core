## Observation

The CIF minimal structural set (four elements)
was recorded as the condition set that interrupts the CIF loop
in an AI narrative-generation system.

The same four structural elements
were also observed to operate
in resolving a CIF-isomorphic pattern
within a non-AI system
in the context of public transportation infrastructure operations.

| Separation of input / output / constraints | input / output / invariant constraints | transmission method / processing volume / manual version |
| Fixation of responsibility location | locus of judgment authority | central judgment unit vs field execution unit |
| Stopping condition | interruption of recursive meta-analysis | stop further recursion after confirming the need for acceleration |

## What This Observation Means

The "human-AI isomorphism" in the CIF specification
describes the isomorphism of failure.

This observation indicates
that the isomorphism may also extend
to failure-interruption conditions.

If failure is isomorphic,
there is a high possibility
that the interruption conditions are also isomorphic.

This document records
a case in which that possibility
was structurally observed.

What this document fixes
is only the observation
of structural reappearance.

That reappearance alone
does not prove cause, scope,
or general applicability.
