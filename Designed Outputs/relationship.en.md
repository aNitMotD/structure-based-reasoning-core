# Relationship Between ZR and ZR-UX

Zero Reflect (ZR) and ZR-UX are not layered by feature expansion.
They are separated by responsibility authority.

## ZR: Core Constraint System

ZR is a responsibility-boundary reflection system.
Its purpose is not to assist decision-making,
but to prevent responsibility transfer to the system.

ZR defines:
- What must never be produced (decisions, advice, conclusions)
- Where responsibility boundaries exist
- Where the system must stop

ZR is intentionally minimal.
Its constraints are absolute and non-negotiable.

## ZR-UX: Boundary Visibility Layer

ZR-UX is a UX layer built on top of ZR.
It does not add permissions, authority, or judgment capability.

ZR-UX exists to answer a single question:
“How much can be shown without violating ZR?”

ZR-UX defines:
- How responsibility boundaries can be made recognizable
- How clarification can occur without guidance
- How stopping or silence can be treated as valid UX outcomes

ZR-UX is strictly subordinate to ZR.

## Authority and Precedence

When documents conflict:
1. ZR core specifications and constraints always take precedence.
2. Misuse checklists and misuse modes override UX behavior.
3. ZR-UX documents never introduce new rules or permissions.
4. Notes and examples are explanatory and non-normative.

ZR-UX may be removed entirely
without altering the correctness of ZR.

## Design Invariant

ZR remains intact even if ZR-UX is removed.
ZR-UX is valid only as long as it does not weaken ZR.

ZR defines the boundary.
ZR-UX only makes the boundary visible.
